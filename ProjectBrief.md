I want to create a phone app (Apple or both) to represent both language learning and more specifically history of the language.

I am looking specifically at Asian languages derived by Chinese. Sino-Xenic Languages - But specifically Mandarin and the influence in Japanese (Kanji) and Korean (Hanja). 

I live in Korea, and I do a lot of work in China and Japan, travelling across the countries, I often come across words that are similar, sentence structures that are the same. Many still use the same symbol, but many have a Koreanized or japanized version of the chinese symmbol, and maybe even the Mandarin is a newer version of the symbol.

Specifically I'm after the symbols that are STILL in use in all 3 languages, or grammar points or other similarities. I am not interested in the symbols/words that are only being used in 1 language but not the other 2. 

I want the app to show the symbol from historical times, as they all used to be actual painting, and can be either split apart Example : "Left representing a hand (meaning) and right representing the sound". Basically I want to disect the Hanja/Kanji/Symbol, to understand it from a more primitive sense, and then apply it to it's current use in all three languages. 

My idea behind this, is that it's very useful for language learning, to understand the meaning behind the symbols, how they are used today, and how they came to be used from ancient time. I also want the users to be able to start picking up either similar sounds or similar symbols across all three languages. 

This is not a "learn a language" type of app, but will be more about learning the history behind the words or structure, and how they are still similar to this day across countries. How they can be applied today, and how we can with understanding these symbols, be able to understand a decent portion of all three of these languages.

It's more about receiving some understanding of all three languages, more so than learning 1. 

I am very interested in learning more Hanja, but also learning more Kanji, and then combining them, and I'm always very happy when I can recognize a word or symbol in another language because I've learned it it one. This is the feeling that we're going for with this app. 


What we need for the app; 
1. We need to do a lot of research into the languages, analyze potentiallyboth Kanji, Hanja and Mandarin to find common denominators. Analyze the top 10 000 words in each language to look for clues and rules that may be similar.
2. I want it to be useful for learners of all three languages, or just interest in the history of these language

As for the actual structure of the app.

1. I want it to be available offline.
2. I want there to be at least 500-1000 lessons of common denominators. Preferably common symbols, but could also be rules, grammar or structure that is common between the three
3. I want for Symbols it to be drawn on the app, as it would be in historical time, and gradually becoming the symbol we can recognize today. (Also the correct stroke patterns)
4. I want the symbols to be disected and shown with real life why they came to be. (Example : The drawing of a hand is "x" symbol, and today it's simply drawn like this "y")
5. It's a language/history learning app, but it should mostly be oriented around 1 lesson (1 symbol/rule) at a time.
6. Every lesson will apply the current (today) word with 2-3 examples from each language. 
7. The lessons will eventually (not needed for part 1) include voice
